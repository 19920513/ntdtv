<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】情报“吸尘器” 中共间谍活动频繁</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2011年5月16日讯】《美联社》在最近发表的长篇调查报导说，很长一段时期以来，中共处心积虑窃取美国机密<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/情报.md#1">情报</a>，而且活动日益频繁，引起美国专家和有关方面的担忧。美国有关当局认为，今天在美国，有许多为中共服务的人特别关注美国的军事和商业情报。而美国议员说，也有中共特工来美国从事政治活动。</p>
<p>《美联社》的系列报导中提到，中共在美国进行间谍活动的一个主要渠道是通过商人，来获得美国限制出口的商品和技术，然后运到中国，做非法出口生意。用这种私人公司在前台开窗口，经营合法生意，已经成了中共获取美国<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/情报.md#1">情报</a>的常用方法和惯例。 </p>
<p>报导谈到了加州洛杉矶商人朱志伟的案子。朱志伟来自北京，后来加入美国籍。 2009年8月3号，洛杉矶的联邦法庭判处他3年4个月的徒刑，罪名是：向北京一家有军工国防背景的公司，出口可用于军事用途的高科技集成电路。法庭文件显示，朱志伟通过设在哈仙达岗的皮包公司，和加州一个朋友的地址，运输这些限制出口到中国的产品，并提供不实的最终用户资料进行间谍活动。 </p>
<p>而另一位在“微软”总公司工作的华裔工程师杨廉，使用的方法和朱志伟差不多。他于今年3月下旬在联邦法庭认罪，承认自己违反了相关法律，向中国出口可用于卫星用途的可编程半导体设备。 </p>
<p>《美联社》的报导还提到了几个非华裔涉案案例。美国的中国问题专家伍尔泽认为，（中共）还招募一些非华裔人员，来进行间谍活动，是一个值得注意的发展苗头。比如印裔美国科学家“格瓦迪亚”或“施莱佛”这样的人。</p>
<p>格瓦迪亚于1968年进入美国著名军工企业Northrop公司，参与设计美国B2隐形轰炸机，具体负责轰炸机推进系统。他因涉嫌向中共军方提供导弹隐形技术和洗钱，今年1月下旬被判刑32年。 </p>
<p>而施莱佛则是土生土长的美国人。他于去年冬天在华盛顿亚历山大镇的联邦法庭认罪，承认试图混入美国中央情报局或国务院，为中共获取机密情报，今年初被联邦法庭判刑4年。 </p>
<p>施莱佛大学毕业后曾在中国工作生活，中文流利，2004年他在上海居住期间，通过找工作广告认识了一位叫阿曼达的女士，阿曼达介绍他认识了唐姓和吴姓先生，而这三人他后来得知，都是中共安全部门特工。这些人对施莱佛说：“如果有可能，我们想让你为我们搞到一些美国机密情报。” </p>
<p>2005年到2010年，有5年时间，施莱佛一直企图得到美国国务院一份外交官工作，或中央情报局秘密情报处的工作。施莱佛承认，在这期间，他和中共情报官员经常联系，分三次得到7万多美元的报酬。 </p>
<p>施莱佛被捕后在法庭陈述时承认，由于对金钱的贪婪自己一时糊涂“误上贼船”。 </p>
<p>美国司法部反间谍处副处长佩拉克认为，为钱驱动的间谍行为有增加的势头。比如，施莱佛仅仅是“申请”进入美国机要单位，远没到达真正传递情报的地步，中共就支付他几万美元。 </p>
<p>而最新分析报告显示，2008年以来，起码有“57名被告因间谍案在联邦法庭被起诉。他们被控试图向中国输送机密情报，敏感技术或商业机密，而中国方面接受情报的，可能是情报单位，可能是国企，也可能是某个个人”。这些被告中，有9人正等待审判，两人已被认定是重犯，其他人大多都已定罪，只有少数还没有判刑。 </p>
<p>一些美国专家声称，中共在设法得到美国情报资料方面高度活跃，已经成了情报“吸尘器”言外之意是他们能吸收到什么就算什么，来者不拒，多多益善。 </p>
<p>新唐人记者王子琦、周天综合报导。</p>
<p>Become A CCP Spy</p>
<p>According to an Associated Press (AP) report <br />the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) <br />has been calculatedly stealing <br />U.S. classified information for a long time, <br />and their increasingly frequent espionage <br />is angering U.S. experts and related people. <br />U.S. authorities believe that many people in U.S. <br />who work for the CCP are typically interested <br />in U.S. military and business information. <br />According to U.S. congressmen, <br />CCP spies also come for political activities. </p>
<p>AP&#8217;s series report states that a main route <br />of CCP&#8217;s espionage in the U.S. is via businessmen, <br />who steal export restricted products &amp; technologies.<br />They send them to China for illegal export business.<br />Operating legal businesses on the surface <br />while doing espionage has become CCP&#8217;s routine <br />to steal U.S. information. </p>
<p>The report mentioned businessman Zhu Zhiwei&#8217;s <br />case in LA, California. Zhu came from Beijing <br />and became U.S. citizen. On August 3, 2009, <br />LA&#8217;s federal court sentenced him to 3 years <br />and 4 months in prison, for exporting hi-tech military <br />integrated circuits to a Beijing corporation <br />with a national defense industry background. <br />According to court documents, Zhu transferred <br />the restricted products via his briefcase company <br />in Hacienda Heights and a friend&#8217;s address in CA, <br />providing false information to do espionage. </p>
<p>Another Chinese-American engineer <br />in Microsoft Corporation, Yang Lian, used the same <br />method as Zhu. In March this year, he confessed <br />to exporting to China programmable semiconductors <br />applicable to satellites, which is against the law.</p>
<p>AP&#8217;s report also mentioned several non-ethnic <br />Chinese cases. A China expert in U.S., Wu Erze, <br />believes that CCP also recruit non-ethnic Chinese <br />to do espionage, like Gowadia, an Indian American, <br />and Shriver. Wu believes this needs attention.</p>
<p>In 1968, Gowadia joined Northrop, a famous U.S. <br />military industry, to design the propulsion system <br />in the B2 Stealth Bomber. <br />He was sentenced to 32 years this January <br />for providing stealth missile technologies <br />to the CCP military, and for money laundering. </p>
<p>Shriver is a Native American. <br />He confessed to the federal court in Alexander, D.C. <br />that he had tried to enter CIA and State Council <br />to steal information for the CCP. He was sentenced <br />to 4 years earlier this year by the Federal Court. </p>
<p>Shriver lived in China after graduating from college <br />and can speak fluent Chinese. In 2004 while living<br />in Shanghai, he met a woman named Amanda <br />through a recruitment ad. Amanda introduced him <br />to Mr. Tang and Mr. Wu, who were CCP spies <br />as Shriver found out later. They asked Shriver: <br />“If possible, we want you to get some <br />classified information from the U.S. for us.”</p>
<p>From 2005 to 2010, Shriver kept trying to become <br />a diplomat in U.S. State Council,and get inside CIA. <br />He admitted that during this period, <br />he contacted CCP&#8217;s intelligence officials frequently <br />and was rewarded over USD70,000 three times. </p>
<p>Shriver confessed to the court after being arrested <br />that he had “boarded a pirate ship” for money. </p>
<p>Pelak, deputy chief of the U.S. Justice Department&#8217;s <br />counterespionage section, believes an increasingly <br />large number of people will do espionage for money. <br />Take Shriver as an example, who was paid<br />tens of thousands for merely applying <br />to U.S. confidential institutes <br />without providing any information in return. </p>
<p>According to the latest reports, since 2008, <br />at least “57 people were accused of espionage <br />by federal courts. They were accused <br />of trying to provide classified information,<br />like sensitive technologies or business intelligence, <br />to China. Those accepting the information in China <br />might be intelligence agencies, state-owned <br />enterprises or individuals.” Among the accused, <br />9 await trials, 2 have been judged as felons, <br />and most of the rest were given prison terms. </p>
<p>Some U.S. experts said that CCP is highly active <br />in stealing U.S. classified information. <br />They believe that it has become<br />an information “collector,” which means they collect <br />as much information as possible, whatever it is. </p>
<p>NTD reporters Wang Ziqi and Zhou Tian</p>
