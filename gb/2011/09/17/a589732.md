<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】央视春晚秋晚何时了 假唱知多少</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2011年9月17日讯】大陆又爆发假唱风波，前几天大陆《央视》举办的中秋晚会，被眼尖的网友踢爆疑似全程假唱。其中，被点名的还包括台湾歌手范玮琪和大陆天后那英，《央视》官方立刻否认，解释是直播过程中卫星信号出现故障，更改传输方式导致不同步，但香港歌手谭咏麟却在镜头前大方承认，当天应主办单位要求，的确是“对嘴”演出。</p>
<p>《央视》中秋晚会“假唱门”越演越烈。歌手谭咏麟日前与那英、容祖儿等多位明星，参加了《央视》在广东江门举行的中秋节文艺晚会，眼尖的观众发现歌手在台上“对嘴”假唱，更有歌手对口型的时候连歌词都唱错。</p>
<p>谭咏麟接受电台访问时被问到“对嘴型”事件，他慨叹“身不由己”，在旁的郑裕玲问谭咏麟，有没有要求唱现场？谭咏麟无奈的表示自己无法提出要求，因为这个中秋晚会是《央视》的两大秀之一，不容有失。</p>
<p>歌手刀郎，与内地女歌手云朵演唱《爱是你我》时，被网友发现连口形也对不上。</p>
<p>前《中国民营》杂志记者、专栏作家刘逸明：“明星假唱在中国是非常普遍的现象，既使在春节联欢晚会上，也有很多明星在假唱，如果是假唱过假，这个危害性就很大了，因为一旦开了这个先例，以后就会有很多人模仿他。”</p>
<p>自从08年北京奥运开幕式，爆出9岁女孩“林妙可”在台上对嘴假唱后，中共当局规定，所有商业活动一定要真唱，2009年10月1号施行《营业性演出管理条例实施细则》，其中提到假唱、假演奏公然违反法律法规，侵犯观众合法权益。</p>
<p>大陆律师李天天：“这个规定本身就有问题，因为你既然是演唱，等于是人家来买票，你告诉别人，我收你的钱，我要唱给你听，而不是我要放录音给你听，所以我认为不要假唱这个事，就相当于不要说假话一样，这是不用什么规定的吧，不应该有这样的规定，如果有规定反而有点奇怪。”</p>
<p>这个《条例》还规定：“演员不得以假唱欺骗观众。演出举办单位不得组织演员假唱。任何单位或者个人不得为假唱提供条件”，“演出举办单位应当派专人对演出进行监督，防止假唱行为的发生”，并规定了相应处罚措施。</p>
<p>刘逸明：“中国的规定是很多，规定也很好，但真正要落实下去，不是一件容易的事，法律上规定与重婚罪一样，事实上犯重婚罪的人太多了，但是没有一个重婚的人被制裁的。”</p>
<p>广电总局局长王太华，在关于“研究部署2009年中央电视台春节联欢晚会”的会议中，要求杜绝“央视春晚假唱”作法。几年下来，“假唱”依旧，广电总局也从未就“央视春晚假唱行动”开出过罚单。</p>
<p>湖北武汉《中国》杂志社记者刘逸明：“假唱也一样啊，每年都有假唱，但是没有哪个明星被制裁啊，但在香港可能就不一样，如果香港的明星在香港假唱，我估计就算没有部门去制裁，后果应该也会很严重，因为香港是一个讲诚信的社会，这个明星一旦让别人觉得你不诚信，你可能在这个地方就没有市场。”</p>
<p>面对各界的质疑，《央视》虽然提出解释，但歌手都承认“对嘴”，主办单位解释再多，似乎也是自打嘴巴。</p>
<p>新唐人记者陈汉、郭敬采访报导。</p>
<p>Lip-Synching at CCTV Mid-Autumn Festival Gala</p>
<p>A lip-synching controversy has erupted in mainland China.</p>
<p>Some sharp-eyed netizens were suspicious of lip-synching<br />at the CCTV Mid-Autumn Festival Gala.<br />Taiwanese singer Fan Fan and mainland China diva Na Ying<br />were included on the netizens’ lip-synching list.<br />CCTV immediately issued an official denial,<br />stating that signal failure transpired while broadcasting via satellite,<br />and the altered transmission caused simultaneous delays.<br />Nevertheless, Hong Kong singer Alan Tam admitted in public<br />that he was really lip-synching that day in order to<br />meet the organizer’s requirements.</p>
<p>CCTV Mid-Autumn Festival Gala &#8220;lip-synch" controversy intensified.<br />Singer Alan Tam, Na Ying, Joey Yung and many other singers took part in the CCTV Mid-Autumn Festival Gala held in Jiangmen, Guangdong.<br />Some sharp-eyed audiences found out the singers were lip-synching on stage.<br />Some singers even “sang” the wrong lyrics.</p>
<p>In a radio interview, Alan Tam lamented that<br />he felt obligated to lip-synch.<br />Sitting next to Alan Tam, Carol Cheng asked him,<br />&#8220;there was no requirement to sing live?” Alan Tam said he had no choice.<br />He couldn’t request that, because the Mid-Autumn Festival Gala<br />is one of the two big CCTV shows. Nothing must go wrong.</p>
<p>The netizens said that when the singers Dao Lang and<br />Yun Duo performed the song “Love is You and Me,” their lips didn’t match the lyrics.</p>
<p>Wuhan&#8217;s &#8220;China" magazine reporter Liu Yiming said,<br />“Stars lip-synching is very common in China;<br />even in the Spring Festival Gala,<br />there are a lot of stars lip-synching as well.<br />Fake singing will cause great problems.<br />Once the precedent has been set, many people will follow suit. &#8220;</p>
<p>Since the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games opening ceremony<br />when 9 year-old Lin Miaoke lip-synched,<br />the CCP authorities ordered that all commercial activities<br />must be sung live.<br />On October 1, 2009, “Detailed Rules for the Implementation<br />of the Regulation on the Administration of Commercial Performance” was enforced.<br />According to the law, lip-synching and fake performing are<br />flagrant violations to the regulations, and the rights and interests of the audience.</p>
<p>Chinese lawyer Tiantian Lee said, “The regulation itself is a problem.<br />Since you perform singing, people buy tickets.<br />You tell them that I receive your money, I will sing for you,<br />rather than play tracked recording.<br />Banning fake singing is like forbidding lying.<br />It doesn’t need such a regulation.<br />It shouldn’t have such a regulation.<br />If there is one, it’s odd. &#8220;</p>
<p>The &#8220;Regulation" also states, &#8220;The actor can not lip-synch<br />to deceive the audience.<br />The organizers of the shows cannot organize<br />the performers to lip-synch.<br />Any unit or individual cannot provide conditions<br />for lip-synching.<br />The organizers should send someone to monitor<br />the show to prevent lip-synching."<br />The &#8220;Regulations" also include corresponding penalties.</p>
<p>Wuhan&#8217;s &#8220;China" magazine reporter Liu Yiming said,<br />“Beijing has a lot of regulations.<br />Regulations are good, but to really implement them<br />is not an easy job.<br />Like bigamy, in fact, there are too many people<br />committing bigamy, but none of them are sanctioned. &#8220;</p>
<p>The secretary of the State Administration of Radio Film and<br />Television (SARFT) Wang Taihua asked<br />CCTV to ban lip-synching in the conference for &#8220;Research<br />and deployment of CCTV 2009 Spring Festival Gala."<br />A few years later, fake singing is still rampant.<br />The SARFT never gives tickets to the CCTV’s “Spring Festival Lip-Synching Gala.”</p>
<p>Wuhan &#8220;China" magazine reporter Liu Yiming said,<br />" Lip-synching happens every year, but no stars are punished.<br />However, it’s different in Hong Kong.<br />If a star in Hong Kong lip-synchs,<br />even though there are no regulations to punish him or her,<br />the result will be serious,<br />because Hong Kong is a society which believes in trust.<br />If the star is distrusting, he won’t have a market there.”</p>
<p>Facing widespread doubt, the CCTV gave its explanation,<br />but the singers all admitted that they did, in fact, lip-synch.<br />It seems that the more the CCTV explains,<br />the more self-contradictory it becomes.</p>
<p>NTD reporter Chen Han and Guo Jing</p>
