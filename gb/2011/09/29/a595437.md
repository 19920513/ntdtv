<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】盗采稀土官商暴发 赣面临生态灾难</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2011年9月29日讯】中国稀土生产占世界总产量的97%，欧盟为减少对中国的依赖，正在储备稀土。而中国也试图降低这种资源的生产量，严管稀土开采。不过，中国江西赣州盗采商户在地方当局的包庇下，疯狂盗采稀土出售，暴利惊人。加上滥采稀土，给当地带来生态灾难。请看报导。</p>
<p>香港《东方日报》28号报导，知情人士透露，今年上半年，在以前经济属于不发达的赣州，就涌现出约百个亿万富翁，千万身家者不计其数，全部是稀土矿主，如今的江西赣州满街都是名车。</p>
<p>赣州人张毅，今年初开始做稀土生意，现在身家已过亿，因身边不少朋友都换了车，他也打算换一辆宝马跑车。</p>
<p>四川环保人士陈云飞：“这些亿万富翁出现了，绝对有亿万富翁的官员。他们（官员）只是没有暴露出来。他们要么是裸官，要么他是把钱存到国外，这肯定是官商勾结这里面有，还有他们（开采者）是当官的代言人。”</p>
<p>稀土的珍贵，源于它的用途广泛，包括冶金、机械、化工、陶瓷、皮革等。当中，冶金尤其重要，只要在钢铁和有色金属中加入小量稀土，就能改善金属性能。</p>
<p>报导说，在江西赣州，一吨矿就3万元成本，可以卖40多万，一车至少可以拉10吨，许多开采者只要干1票，就可以赚4百多万。有消息指出，许多稀土矿都有地方官员的股份，每年分红数额惊人。</p>
<p>江西地质矿产部门林先生：“因为受这种利益驱使，由于稀土的价格在上涨，但是，你这种，因为稀土的覆层面积非常广泛，而且开采也非常容易，第三个它的利益啊，受利益的驱使，它确实会存在。”</p>
<p>另外，由于土法开采及提炼稀土金属，手法粗暴简单，对周边生态环境造成无可补救的严重侵害。当地农民多次要求当局严查，但都不了了之。目前，已有大批农民因为环境被污染，土地无法耕作而要出外打工。</p>
<p>四川环保人士陈云飞表示，这样没有规划、没有计划的开采，牺牲了几代人的利益。</p>
<p>陈云飞：“它是牺牲了几代人的利益。一个是环保、一个环境利益，再一个是财富利益。它所有项目，它都没有这个正规的，就是方方面面的专家论证过后。比如说，专业论证过后这个稀土，我们必须做长远的规划，今年开采多少，明年开采多少，它没有搞这个，它就是看眼前的利润。”</p>
<p>在赣州龙南县，因稀土资源开采，导致生态破坏尤其恶劣，随处可见红褐色、寸草不生的裸露山体，清涧和河流已被污染成黄浊不堪的臭水，人畜灌溉皆不能用，农民只好蓄雨水备用。至于被酸水浸蚀过的农地，再也无法耕种。</p>
<p>江西环保工作人员：“但是我们就知道，它就是氨氮一定会超标，然后生态一定会破坏，地下水也肯定会受到影响。”</p>
<p>赣州有色冶金研究所副所长杨新华表示，整治稀土，迫在眉睫。</p>
<p>新唐人记者唐睿、柏妮采访报导。</p>
<p>Illegal Mining Reaps Millions for Jiangxi Officials</p>
<p>While the rest of the China is trying to limit rare earth mining by applying stricter rules to its production,<br />in Ganzhou City, Jiangxi Province, officials are collaborating<br />with business owners to illegally mine and sell rare earths,<br />reaping huge profits and brining ecological disaster to the area.</p>
<p>Hong Kong’s Oriental Daily News reported on September 28,<br />that according to sources, in the first half of 2011,<br />there were around a hundred billionaires<br />in the economically undeveloped city of Ganzhou,<br />including a multitude of millionaires, all of them being<br />rare earth mining business owners.<br />They all drive luxury cars and can be spotted everywhere in the city.</p>
<p>Zhang Yi, from Ganzhou City, started a rare earth business<br />earlier this year and already has a net worth in the billions.<br />Many of his friends have already upgraded their cars,<br />so he is considering buying a new BMW.</p>
<p>Sichuan environmental activist, Chen Yunfei:<br />&#8220;If business owners are becoming billionaires, there must be<br />government officials who are also becoming billionaires;<br />they’re just not making it obvious.<br />The business owners are paying off officials,<br />or they’re diverting the payback money abroad.<br />These business owners are definitely colluding with officials<br />and are acting as the voice of government officials."</p>
<p>Rare earths are valuable because of their versatility,<br />as they can be used in making metals, machine parts,<br />chemicals, ceramics, and leather.<br />Their most important use is in metallurgy, as adding a small<br />amount of rare earths to steel and nonferrous metals<br />can greatly improve their properties.</p>
<p>According to a report, in Ganzhou City, the cost of one ton<br />of rare earth is about 30,000 yuan (US$4,691),<br />but it can be sold for over 400,000 yuan (US$62,558).<br />One truck can carry at least 10 tons, so just one load<br />can net about 4 million yuan (US$625,492).<br />According to sources, local officials are shareholders<br />in these rare earth mining companies, reaping huge annual dividends.</p>
<p>Mr. Lin from the Department of Geology and Mineral Resources<br />in Jiangxi Province said, &#8220;Driven by financial interest,<br />the price of rare earths keeps rising.<br />Since rare earths have many industrial uses,<br />they can be easily mined, bringing fortunes to those involved."</p>
<p>Also, because the mining and refining process of rare earths<br />is generally being carried out in a careless manner,<br />irreparable damages is being done to the surrounding areas.<br />Local farmers have repeatedly requested that authorities perform<br />thorough investigations into the situation, but to no avail.<br />Many farmers are forced to perform hard labor in other cities<br />because their farm land is contaminated beyond repair.</p>
<p>Sichuan environmental activist, Chen Yunfei, said,<br />&#8220;Mining without a plan will affect many generations to come."</p>
<p>Chen: &#8220;It is at expense and interest of many future generations.<br />On one hand we are concerned about protecting the environment,<br />on the other hand, financial gains are at stake.<br />No mining project is ever evaluated by experts.<br />When mining rare earths, a business’’ operation needs<br />to be carefully evaluated and must go through long-term planning.<br />We have to limit the amount that can be mined each year.<br />No one does this, because all they care about is immediate profits."</p>
<p>In Ganzhou City, in Longnan County, the ecological damage<br />done there due to rare earth mining, has turned the soil red,<br />with barren hills spotted everywhere.<br />The once clear river has turned yellowish<br />with an unbearable strong smell, thus the river water cannot be used<br />to feed humans or animals.<br />Farmers can only stock up on rainwater.<br />After the farm land is exposed to acidic erosion,<br />it can no longer be used to grow food.</p>
<p>Jiangxi environmental staff: &#8220;We know that the ammonia content<br />is over the allowable limit, thus damaging the environment<br />and affecting underground water sources."</p>
<p>Ganzhou Nonferrous Metallurgy Research Institute,<br />deputy director, |Yang Xinhua said environmental remediation<br />of rare earths is imminent.</p>
<p>NTD reporters Tang Rui and Bo Ni.</p>
