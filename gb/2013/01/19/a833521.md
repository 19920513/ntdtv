<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】宁愿“扫大街”死在编制里的硕士生</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2013年01月19日讯】近年来，在中国号称所谓“国考”的国家<ahref="https://github.com/1992513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/focus/公务员">公务员</a>考试热的烫手，报考人数逐年攀升。而最近一则硕士生应聘哈尔滨环卫工的新闻，也是热的烫手。对于具有专业知识的研究生，却立志于月收入仅仅1500元的环卫工人职位，是就业的压力﹖还是硕士生贬值了。有分析指出，其实环卫工作是跳板，当官才是目地。事实果真如此吗？让我们听听社会专家和学生他们怎么说。</p>
<p>落选哈尔滨环卫工招考的硕士生王洋说，“就算是死，我也要死在编制里。”死在编制里，成为最近最热的网络流行语之一。</p>
<p>落选研究生的一句话引起千层浪，中国高学历研究生的就业问题再度浮出台面。有人质疑，难道十年寒窗苦读，一切就是为了“扫大街”？事实上，很多高学历的学生眼里，只要是带编制的工作，即使是做清洁工，也在所不惜。</p>
<p>据调查，2013届毕业生最愿意去的单位是国有企业。</p>
<p>深圳当代社会观察研究所所长刘开明：“这确实反映了现在非常大的社会问题，那么多的精英都往体制跑，原因在于体制，还有机关<ahref="https://github.com/1992513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/focus/公务员">公务员</a>，有些编制的事业单位的人员，他们的工资福利非常好，而且还有很多灰色、甚至黑色的收入，这样就导致了很多的年轻人趋之若鹜，从个案来说，确实是很严重的，因为他所受的高等教育完全是荒废了。”</p>
<p>“深圳当代社会观察研究所”所长刘开明认为，这个现象反映了中共官员选拔方面有问题，整个体制内的政府和事业单位，各种管理阶层人员的提拔，实际上是不对社会上开放的，而是通过体制内部提拔，这才是编制的最大诱惑！</p>
<p>刘开明：“实际上也是他进了这个编制，他受过高等教育硕士学位的人，很容易能够升迁，他做环卫工人不会超过三年，就有机会升到基层的领导，甚至中高层领导，这样的话，他所谓死要死在编制里，显然不是死也要做编制内的环卫工人，而是要进入里面做官。”</p>
<p>网路转载“武大学长的一封信”，信中提到，就业一直是中国大学生的敏感话题，而现在充斥校园的是，对中国教育的反感，对中国体制的漫骂，对自己前途的没有信心。</p>
<p><ahref="https://github.com/1992513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/focus/大陆">大陆</a>应届研究生陈先生：“研究生的学费也挺贵的，但是整个对社会来说，价值都没体现出来，应该有一个规划，我感觉应该制订一个详细的规划，每年大概能需要多少，然后我招多少，就业的时候怎样去分配，怎样去刺激这些人去就业。”</p>
<p>这位<ahref="https://github.com/1992513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/focus/大陆">大陆</a>陈姓应届研究所学生说，政府没有规划，每年录取这么多的研究生，既浪费国家资源，又浪费学生的时间，连带整个社会的生产能力和运作能力都下降。</p>
<p>陈先生：“你看有的人学马克思主义哲学，这种专业研究生，你说这种专业能干嘛﹖什么样的企业需要你这种专业研究生？或者社会主义建设，这样的研究生除了考公务员，你还能干嘛﹖国家就是为了收学费，然后死命死命招，基本上想考的话，你随便都能考上，然后就业时候就不一定了，真的没什么工作。”</p>
<p>根据统计显示，从1994年到2000年期间，累计参加公务员考试的人数大约4万多人。自2009年以后，中国每年参加公务员考试的年轻人超过100万人，2013年报考人数更是达到156万人。</p>
<p>据了解，哈尔滨市举行环卫系统公开招聘人员，录取的448名环卫工人当中，共有7名是研究生学历。</p>
<p>网友感叹：为了一个研究生，人们起早贪黑的苦读书，而等拿到了研究生，又要起早贪黑去扫地，知识就这么被灰尘淹没了&#8230;&#8230;。</p>
<p>采访/朱智善编辑/黄亿美 后制/郭敬</p>
<p>China’s Postgraduates: “Dying for Getting Government Jobs"</p>
<p>In recent years, the civil service exam in China is become<br />very popular, with a growing number of applicants yearly.<br />This was also seen in the recent recruitment<br />of sanitation workers in the city of Harbin.<br />This low paid job has seen heavy competition,<br />even among graduates with Masters’ degrees.<br />Is this a reflection of employment pressures<br />or is it the devaluation of Master’s degrees?<br />Observers think that the sanitation<br />worker job acts as a springboard.<br />The goal of the well-educated <br />applicants is to become officials.<br />Let’s look at what is happening in today’s China.</p>
<p>Wang Yang, an excluded candidate for Harbin’s <br />sanitation worker recruitment, is a Master’s graduate.<br />He said, “I would even die to become<br />a government institution employee.”<br />Thus, a new term was created, that <br />of “dying for getting government jobs".</p>
<p>Nationwide public concern is growing towards employment<br />issues for China’s well-educated job seekers. <br />Some question why graduates, after years of hard<br />study, are competing to be street sweepers? <br />In reality, lots of well-educated applicants in China think <br />it is worthwhile to get any public service job, even a cleaner.</p>
<p>Reportedly, the most attractive workplace for 2013<br />college graduates are state-owned enterprises.</p>
<p>Liu Kaiming, Director of a civil think tank in Shenzhen:<br />“It really mirrors a big social problem. <br />So many elites are seeking government jobs <br />now, and the root cause is the political system.<br />Another reason is that these civil servants earn enviable <br />salaries and benefits, plus gray and under-the-table incomes.<br />That’s why state jobs can attract so many young people.</p>
<p>In this recent case, I think being a street cleaner <br />makes the young man discard his good education.”</p>
<p>Liu Kaiming says this fact reflects a flawed <br />mechanism for the regime’s official selection.<br />In China, public service job openings and opportunities <br />for managerial promotion are actually behind closed doors.<br />All are internally selected, which is the greatest attraction.</p>
<p>Liu Kaiming: “If he enters a government institution, <br />he can easily get promotion with his master’s degree.<br />He’ll work as a cleaner for no more than three years.</p>
<p>But he’ll gain a chance to be promoted as a grass-roots<br />leader, or even to a middle-level or high-level leader.<br />So his claim of dying for getting a government job<br />refers to the chance of being a leader.<br />It is not for being a cleaner forever.”</p>
<p>A letter from a Wuhan University graduate <br />is being widely circulated on the internet.<br />It said that employment issues have long been <br />a sensitive topic among China’s college students.<br />Now college campuses are filled with resentment toward <br />China’s education, and abuse of China’s political system.<br />Students are despairing at their own future. </p>
<p>Mr. Chen, Masters student in China: <br />“Postgraduate tuition fees are quite high. <br />However, the value hasn’t <br />really been recognized in society.<br />I think the authorities should make a detailed plan on <br />graduate student enrolment and their job placements.”</p>
<p>Mr. Chen says the flood of unplanned graduate student <br />enrolment wasted national resources, and students’ time.<br />This has resulted in low social <br />productivity and operating capability.</p>
<p>Mr. Chen: “Such as enrollments <br />to study the philosophy of Marxism.<br />What kind of enterprise needs graduates with such a major?<br />Another example is the major in Socialist Construction. <br />Besides applying for civil service employment, <br />what else can these graduates do after graduation?<br />In order to collect money, the authorities’ graduate<br />student enrollment has been over subscribed. <br />It’s easy to get enrolled, but once a student<br />has graduated, no jobs await them.” </p>
<p>Public data shows that in the period 1994-2000, over <br />40,000 people in China sat civil service examinations.<br />Since 2009, the number of applicants soared to over 1 million.<br />In 2013, over 1.5 million people applied for civil service jobs.</p>
<p>Media reported that among Harbin’s 448 sanitation<br />worker recruits, there are seven graduate students.</p>
<p>A netizen commented in a post, “In order to get a <br />Master’s degree, students have to toil in their studies.<br />But when they have it, they’ll toil again to sweep the <br />streets. Their knowledge, in this way, is buried in dust…”</p>
