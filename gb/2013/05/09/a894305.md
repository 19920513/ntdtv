<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】美“中国军力报告” 还击中共黑客 ?</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2013年05月09日讯】美国国防部5月6号，发布“2013年<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/中国.md#1">中国</a><ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/军力.md#1">军力</a>和防务发展报告书”。报告除重点介绍中国空军和海军力量增长，还曝光了中国去年的军费支出，数字比北京公布的高出许多。除此之外，美国国防部首次公开指责中国黑客攻击。美国对中共进行的网路攻击，要展开还击了吗？请看专家的分析。</p>
<p>美国国防部本周一，在华盛顿发表了一份将近100页的报告。报告中说，在过去几年，北京发起了持续长久的黑客攻击活动，目地是更加全面的了解美国的外交政策及<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/军力.md#1">军力</a>。</p>
<p>报告说：“2012年，世界各地的大量计算机系统，包括美国政府名下的系统，持续成为侵入目标，其中一些看来可直接归咎于<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/中国.md#1">中国</a>（共）政府和军方。”</p>
<p>华盛顿之前曾呼吁北京，应该对偷窃网络信息的行为加以管制。但当时的美方代表只提到涉及经济领域的黑客袭击。这是美国政府在历史上第一次“明确指责”中共对美国发起网路攻击。</p>
<p>美国《中国事务》杂志主编伍凡：“中共的网络攻击，它并不想破坏你，它首先目前的目标，不是破坏你，而是想要偷你的东西。”</p>
<p>据了解，美国遭受的网路间谍活动有90%源自中国，但这次五角大楼向国会提交的年度“中国军力报告”中，如此直白的指责，非比寻常。</p>
<p>美国网络安全公司(Mandiant)曾表示，位于上海浦东的61398部队，可能是中国大陆官方的“黑客部队”。美国中文杂志《中国事务》主编伍凡表示，美国在几年前，开始对中共的黑客活动提出警告，但是，中共不里不睬，继续攻击和偷袭情报。伍凡说，美国可能开始反击了。</p>
<p>伍凡：“现在美国人已经受够了，不耐烦了，已经知道你再这样做下去，对它警告是没有用的。所以美国人下一步一定会反击。现在这次2013 年的国防部对美国国会的军事报告里边，我看明明白白的指名道姓的提出来，就是下一步要开始还击了，这个还击，美国手段是足够的，所以一旦反击起来，中国它能不能受得了，我不知道。” </p>
<p>美国《华盛顿邮报》5月6号也报导说，五角大楼发出美国政府对北京至今为止最清晰的公开指控。</p>
<p>美国国家安全局(National Security Agency)局长基思．亚历山大 (Keith Alexander) 上将，同时兼任军方快速壮大的“网路战”司令部指挥官。他最近告诉国会，他正在创立12个以上的网路攻击部门，必要时针对外国的计算机网路发动攻击。</p>
<p>美国国防部的报告指出，中共发动网路间谍行为的目地是，为中国的武器以及技术行业收集美国军备的信息，同时，窥探美国政府在诸多中国问题上的立场，并了解美国是怎样看待中国军队。报告指称，通过黑客攻击，中国的军事战略家们能够对美国的军力有所了解，而这些信息“在危机爆发时则能够派上用场”。</p>
<p>此外，报告中还披露，中国去年的军费支出在1020亿到1630亿欧元之间，比北京公布的官方数字要高出许多。中共当局今年3月公布的军费才900亿欧元。</p>
<p>相对于中国公布的数字来讲，伍凡认为美国公布的数字更接近实际。</p>
<p>伍凡：“我个人认为可能还不止这个，因为中国这么样的大力发展海面、海上以及陆地、空中太空，整个立体，再包括网路，它全面的来进行推动发展，它不仅仅是军事工业，还发展它的材料，还包括交通，运输，你要去挖山坑，储藏武器”</p>
<p>近年来，中国大陆军费问题一直受到外界的关注和质疑，国际社会普遍认为，中共的实际军费开销，应该是公布的国防预算的两倍到三倍。</p>
<p>采访编辑/常春 后制/李勇</p>
<p>United States Set to Counterattack China&#8217;s Hackers</p>
<p>On May 6th, the U.S. Department of Defense published<br />a report on China&#8217;s military power and defense in 2013.<br />It highlighted the increase in China&#8217;s air and naval forces,</p>
<p>including its military spending,<br />which was much higher than that in Beijing&#8217;s report.<br />In addition, the U.S. Department of Defense publicly<br />accused China&#8217;s hackers of cyber-attacks, for the first time.<br />Will the US be fighting back against China&#8217;s cyber-attacks?<br />—Let&#8217;s see the expert analyses.</p>
<p>The U.S. Department of Defense in Washington<br />has released a near 100-page report saying that<br />Beijing has been launching long-term hacking activities<br />for years,<br />to get a comprehensive understanding of U.S. foreign policy<br />and military power.</p>
<p>It reported: &#8220;In 2012, a large number of computer systems<br />worldwide, including many of the U.S. government systems,<br />have been targeted by hackers; some of which seemed to<br />directly come from the Chinese government and military."</p>
<p>Washington had previously called on Beijing<br />to control the hacking activities of network information.<br />However, the U.S. representative at the time<br />referred only to attacks of the economic field.<br />Now, for the first time in the history, the U.S. government<br />has explicitly accused the Chinese Communist Party (CCP)<br />of launching cyber-attacks on the United States.</p>
<p>[Wu Fan, Editor-in-Chief of Chinese affairs]:<br />&#8220;The goal of the CCP&#8217;s network attacks is not to destroy<br />its target, but to steal from it."</p>
<p>It&#8217;s understood that 90% of internet espionage<br />in the U.S. has come from China,<br />but such a blatant accusation in the annual China Military<br />Power Report is unprecedented.</p>
<p>Network security company Mandiant says, troops in Pudong,<br />Shanghai, are likely to be mainland official hackers.<br />Wu Fan says, the US began warning the CCP<br />regarding hacking activities some years ago<br />but the CCP have ignored the warnings and have continued,<br />so the United States may now begin fighting back.</p>
<p>[Wu Fan]: &#8220;Americans have had enough and<br />they already know that<br />the Chinese will keep attacking—warnings are useless;<br />the next step for the Americans will be to fight back.<br />Now, in the U.S. military report to the Congress,<br />the Department of Defense has openly named the offender.<br />This means that the US is ready to fight back<br />and they will be very sufficient in counterattacking.<br />Once this cyber war begins,<br />China will not be able to stand it."</p>
<p>On May 6th, the Washington Post also reported that<br />the Pentagon had issued its most clear public accusation against Beijing.</p>
<p>Admiral Keith Alexander, the director of the U.S. National<br />Security Agency and commander-in-chief of the cyber war,<br />told Congress that he&#8217;d established over 12 network attack<br />departments to counterattack foreign networks, if necessary.</p>
<p>The U.S. Department of Defense report says that<br />the goal of the CCP&#8217;s network espionage is to collect<br />information on U.S. armaments, weapons, and its technology industry.<br />Meanwhile, the CCP wants to understand the U.S.&#8217;position<br />on many issues and their thoughts about the Chinese army.<br />Through cyber-attacks, China&#8217;s military strategists<br />are trying to gain understanding of the U.S. military power,<br />which will come in handy when there&#8217;s a crisis."</p>
<p>The report also reveals that China&#8217;s 2012 military spending<br />was between 102-billion to 163-billion Euros;<br />much higher than the figures Beijing announced this March,<br />which claimed the spending to be only 90-billion Euros.<br />Wu Fan believes that the figures released<br />by the United States are much closer to reality.</p>
<p>[Wu Fan]: &#8220;I think the figure may be even higher than that<br />China has vigorously developed its sea, land and air space,<br />including networks—such a comprehensive development<br />isn&#8217;t limited to the military industry but<br />includes development of material, trafficking,<br />transportation and cave-digging to store weapons."</p>
<p>In recent years, the issue of mainland China&#8217;s<br />military spending has concerned the outside world.<br />The international community largely believes that the CCP&#8217;s<br />actual military spending is 2 to 3 times higher than it claims.</p>
