<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】市长调离“烂尾工程”谁来负责？</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2013年02月20日讯】山西省大同市连续多天出现万民聚集街头，联名请愿，挽留即将调离的大同市长耿彦波。寒风中，随处可见民众举牌、拉横幅的身影，甚至在耿彦波离任当天，数百民众集体下跪再次恳请他留下。这些照片很快在网络流传，网民惊叹“两大奇迹”：一是中共居然有官被“万民爱戴”；二是万人游行居然没有警察身影。为什么出现这个情况呢? 请看本台记者的报导。</p>
<p>2月8号和2月12号，山西大同市民两次发起大规模“万人签名留人”活动，来“挽留”即将调任的市长耿彦波。顿时，大同市成为了舆论关注的焦点，但很快又有消息传出，所谓的万民挽留活动，其实是当地政府自导自演的一出戏。</p>
<p>山西大同市民王建：“全是一个政府的宣传，市政府宣传部搞出来的一些东西。”</p>
<p>耿彦波自从2008年任职以来，就强力推行推倒原有一切建筑，重新打造“大同古城”计划，6年耗资600亿，一度引发巨大争议。因为大规模拆迁，耿彦波被当地百姓称为“耿拆拆”或“造城市长”。而轰轰烈烈的“古城再造计划”尚未完成，耿彦波就接到任职太原市长的调令。</p>
<p>时事评论员邢天行：“因为中共它体制本身就是领导干部到哪个地方，一个人说了算，一拍脑袋一热，用我的铁腕推行政策，实际上，他在承建的过程当中，因为大量的投资借债啊，包括各方面协调设计，所有合同这些东西，如果他走了的话，这些能不能完成﹖就很难讲了。”</p>
<p>当地民众表示，百姓们挽留耿彦波，并非报导中说的对他如何“留恋和感激”，真正的原因是他们担心耿彦波调离之后，负债百亿的改建工程“烂尾”结果，将由他们承担巨额债务。另一方面也担忧相关的拆迁赔偿、安置、工程款支付等事宜会被无限拖延。</p>
<p>王建：“光我个人来说，我都说不能让他走，为什么呢﹖两百几十亿的外债，这是已经人们基本都清楚了，而且这么多大工程，全都是处于没完工的阶段，我被分到的安置房，质量差不说了，到现在都欠了多少钱的尾款不给。”</p>
<p>山东大学退休教授孙文广：“有些官员他没有长期的规划、长期的打算，他只想火红一阵子，干一段很火红，但是以后银行的钱谁来还呢﹖这都是问题。”</p>
<p>据大陆媒体报导，耿彦波的继任者李俊明，为了化解大同市民对耿彦波离任的担忧，发表了“五个凡是”，大概内容是：凡是正在进行的工程会加快进程；凡是被征收房屋的住户会妥善安置；凡是已经开工的政府工程要按进度拨款。有媒体解读，新任市长的承诺，恰恰证明了耿彦波在以上方面都存在问题。</p>
<p>邢天行：“这个市长他的存在和老百姓目前自身利益是息息相关，他一旦要是走了，老百姓利益这些得不到实施，下边人来了呢，上边人做那些，我做不了，至少是要打折扣的，甚至很多时候衔接不上，这个东西你找不着责任。所以这个时候当他们听说当官的准备调离的时候，最切实的感受就是一定要想办法，逼政府作出一个承诺。”</p>
<p>山东大学退休教授孙文广曾经担任“中国经济协会”理事，他认为，山西大同“古城再造”计划，是“政绩冲动”下的产物，投资巨大且有风险，很可能血本无归。孙文广说，吃亏的只能是老百姓，而这一点，百姓也意识到了，所以才会如此挽留他们的“父母官”，因为他们不想在家园被毁的同时，还要替政府买单。</p>
<p>采访/田净 编辑/张天宇 后制/钟元</p>
<p>Who Responsible For Mayor’s Unfinished Project?</p>
<p>More than 10,000 people in Datong City in Shanxi went on<br />the street to plea for mayor Geng Yanbuo to stay.<br />In the chilly weather, people were everywhere with <br />placards and banners in their hands.<br />Residents even kneeled down to plea for Geng’s stay.</p>
<p>Photos of the scene immediately circulated online,<br />Netizens said these are “two miracles”: <br />One is the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) official who<br />was loved by tens of thousands of citizens. <br />Another surprise is that no police came to stop the event.<br />Why does the situation appear to be like this? Let’s take a look.</p>
<p>From Feb. 8th to 12th, Datong residents held two events of <br />signature collection, trying to stop mayor Geng Yanbo’s leaving.<br />Meanwhile, Datong city became public focus.</p>
<p>However, news spread that the so-called citizens “plea for <br />mayor staying event” was manipulated by local government.</p>
<p>Wang Jian, a Tatong citizen: “All are government’s<br />propagandas. The city council designed it all.”</p>
<p>Geng Yanbo took the office in 2008, since then,<br />he implemented a series plan:<br />To demolish all the old buildings, re-creating a new city of<br />“Ancient Datong”. It will cost 60 billion yuan in six years.<br />It aroused enormous controversy.</p>
<p>Because of the large-scale of demolition, local people called<br />Geng “Geng Caicai” (Cai means demolition) or “creating city mayor”.<br />Yet “re-creating city” project hasn’t completed, Geng received<br />an order transferring him to be a vice mayor in Taiyuan city.</p>
<p>Xing Tianxing, Current Affairs Commentator: “The nature of<br />CCP’s system is that, cadres are in charge wherever they go.<br />Once they are hot-headed, they will use their rights to <br />make a new policy. <br />In fact, when Geng began the project, it involved massive <br />investment and loans, including various aspects of design and<br />coordination, and all contracts etc. If he left,<br />can the project be carried on? It is hard to say.”</p>
<p>Local people said that they want to stop Geng leaving,<br />wasn’t as media reported that they love and appreciate Geng’s achievement.<br />The real reason is that they worried after Geng leaving,<br />the billion yuan debt will be on local people’s shoulders.<br />In addition, they are worried that compensation of demolition,<br />resettlement and project payment will be infinitely delayed.</p>
<p>Wang Jian: “Even I don’t want him to go. Why?<br />There is more than 20 billion yuan debt.<br />People are clear about it. In addition, such a huge project,<br />is still not completed. <br />I was assigned a poor quality house, the balance money<br />they owed me will never be seen.”</p>
<p>Sun Wenguang, a retired Professor from Shandong University:<br />“Some officials didn’t have long-term plan, they just want to <br />be famous in a short time. But who will pay back the load to <br />the banks? These are the problems.”</p>
<p>Media in Mainland China reported, Geng Yanbo’s successor<br />Li Junming issued a notice to defuse residents’ worries of Geng leaving.<br />The notice said “All the on-going projects<br />will speed up the process.<br />All people whose houses had been demolished<br />will re-arrange accommodation.<br />All the government project will assign the funds<br />according to the process of the project.<br />Some media understood that the new mayor’s promises proved<br />during Geng’s term these problems already existed.</p>
<p>Xing Tianxing: “Mayor staying or not is directly connected <br />with citizens’ interests.<br />If he walks away, citizens’ interest can’t be implemented.</p>
<p>For the next mayor, he can find excuses that<br />what former mayor did, I can’t.<br />At least, there is a gap, most time nothing connects,<br />no one takes responsibility.<br />Thus, once citizens heard the mayor will leave, they will find<br />a way to pressure the government to make a promise.”</p>
<p>Sun Wenguang served as a director of the China<br />Economic Association.<br />He believes Datong’s project was the product of pursuing<br />political achievement. The investment was huge and risky.<br />It is possible they will lose everything. Sun said that only<br />citizens will lose, this point is realized by the citizens.<br />Thus they tried to make their “parent” official stay.</p>
<p>As they don’t want to pay for the government,<br />meanwhile, losing their homes and land.</p>
