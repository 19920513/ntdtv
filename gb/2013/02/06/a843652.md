<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】假外资和非法资金外流 掏空中国？</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2013年02月06日讯】多年来，中国经济吸引了大量资本涌入，最近资本外逃真相也在徐徐展开。中国的一个研究机构表明，每年60%的GDP被外国资本拿走。另外据中纪委统计，中国资金非法外流持续升级，去年的非法<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/资金外流.md#1">资金外流</a>已突破1万亿美元，国际组织报导，那些流到海外的资金又以境外直接投资的名义流回中国，享受政府给予的税收优惠，从而使中国遭受双重的打击。专家认为，中国可能存在被掏空的危险。</p>
<p>中国媒体1月18号援引中纪委的内部通报说，据不完全统计，2010年中国大陆非法<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/资金外流.md#1">资金外流</a>4,120亿美元，2011年达到6,000亿美元，而2012年已突破1万亿美元，预计2013年的非法资金外流规模将达到1万5千亿美元。</p>
<p>美国“南卡罗来纳大学艾肯商学院”教授谢田指出，中国资金非法外流除了外流时造成的直接损失外，还存在另外两方面的财富流失。</p>
<p>美国南卡罗来纳大学艾肯商学院教授谢田：“然后再冒称外国投资来返回中国，可以利用税务、土地转让方面的优惠，实际上对中国经济来讲受到了第二次损失，当然这些投资如果生产了什么东西，盈利以后再度的转移到海外，社会的财富又损失第三次。”</p>
<p>如果生产的产品用于出口，还可以利用退税政策再一次对中国经济进行盘剥。谢田进一步指出，这些资金对中国财富造成多次流血的同时，还会对中国经济造成混乱。</p>
<p>谢田：“当然他这些钱去投资中国的时候，对中国的GDP有影响——虚假的、人为的抬高了中国GDP的数字，这样对中国经济有影响，实际上是一种欺骗性的影响。”</p>
<p>《美国之音》报导，中国的资金外流主要采取以下几种方式：在离岸金融中心设立公司，直接携带现金出境，进出口报假账，以及其他隐秘的转账办法。</p>
<p>谢田说，国内企业在国外购买资产时，以高于10倍的价格虚报资产价值，再与中共高官合谋贷款，造成国有银行资金流出海外，也时有发生。另外以购买专利的方式，使资产流出，也是常见的办法。</p>
<p>有数据显示，中国新领导人习近平上台后，中国的贪官更是加紧了卷款外逃的步伐。</p>
<p>谢田：“除了对中共的权力进行有效的监督，就像由各种会计组织、法律体制、新闻媒体来共同监督的话很难杜绝这一点，体制没有改变的话，我想流失会继续，并且很可能会加剧，随着看到中共的垮台，破产在即的时候，这些贪官处于恐惧的心理，出于保全自己财富的心理，会加速这种资金的外流。”</p>
<p>“复旦大学”沉丁立教授对《美国之音》表示，中国在流血，中国人转移出去的资金还是少部分，而外国资本每年从中国拿走GDP的60%。</p>
<p>著名经济学家何清涟在一篇文章中探讨了“假外资”的内幕。何清涟指出，长期以来，中国引进的外资当中，有很大一部分是假外资，就是中国人或公司将资金转到海外，在国外注册公司后，再以“外资”的名义回流中国。</p>
<p>据中共政府部门总结，“假外资”主要有三种类型：第一种是在港澳及国外有实体经营的中资企业，出于发展的需要，回国创办“外资”企业﹔第二种是出于海外融资的目地，通过注册海外空壳公司及返程收购，以“红筹形式上市”的原内资企业﹔第三种是纯粹出于政策性寻租的目地，到境外尤其是离岸金融中心注册空壳企业，然后将自己变身为外企的原内资企业。</p>

<p>China&#8217;s Fake Foreign Investments</p>
<p>For many years China has attracted an influx of capital.<br />Recently, China&#8217;s capital outflow is gradually escalating.<br />Chinese research institution showed that 60% of China&#8217;s<br />annual GDP goes away as a foreign capital.<br />Central Committee for Discipline Inspection&#8217;s data shows<br />China&#8217;s illegal capital outflow continues to increase and has exceeded $1 trillion last year.<br />Reports of international organizations indicate, the outflow<br />of funds comes back to China as direct foreign investments, and enjoys government&#8217;s tax incentives.<br />China is subjected to suffering from both perspectives.<br />Thus experts think China faces the danger of being hollowed.</p>
<p>On January 18, Chinese media cited internal announcement<br />of the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection.<br />According to incomplete statistics,<br />in 2010, illegal capital outflow was $412 billion,<br />in 2011, was $600 billion, exceeding $1 trillion in 2012.<br />It is expected this number will be $1.5 trillion in 2013.</p>
<p>University of South Carolina&#8217; Aiken Business School<br />Professor Xie Tian thinks,<br />China&#8217;s illegal capital outflow causes two others<br />damages in addition to the loss of wealth.</p>
<p>Xie Tian: “They return to China as foreign investments,<br />and take advantage of tax, land transfer benefits, etc.,<br />which actually cause the 2nd damage to China.<br />Of course, if these investments produce something,<br />the earnings will be transferred to overseas again, causing wealth loss for the third time.”</p>
<p>If the goods manufactured are for exports, they can take<br />advantage of the tax rebate policy and exploit China again.<br />Xie Tian further pointed out that while these investments<br />cause multiple exploitation to China&#8217;s wealth, they also cause confusion to China&#8217;s economy.</p>
<p>Xie Tian: “Of course these money have an impact on China&#8217;s<br />GDP. It falsely raises the GDP. It is actually deceptive.”</p>
<p>VOA reported that China&#8217;s capital outflow<br />usually follows the same scenario.<br />Namely, leaving the country as cash; setting up a company<br />in an offshore financial center; faking import-export books;<br />exploring other secret ways for transferring money.</p>
<p>Xie said, often when Chinese companies buy assets abroad,<br />they report a price 10 times higher than their real value.<br />They conspire with the Chinese Communist Party (CCP)<br />senior officials to transfer money overseas.<br />Another way is to purchase patents,<br />which is also a common approach.</p>
<p>Data shows after the new CCP leader Xi Jinping took power,<br />CCP&#8217;s corrupt officials accelerated their money transfers.</p>
<p>Xie Tian: “Except for effective supervision on CCP&#8217;s power<br />with central accounting system, legal system,<br />and the media, it is hard to put an end to the capital outflow.</p>
<p>I think the loss will continue and will exacerbate.</p>
<p>Along with the collapse of the CCP, corrupt officials<br />will accelerate the outflow of funds out of fear.”</p>
<p>Fudan University&#8217; Professor Chen Dingli told VOA,<br />that China is &#8216;bleeding.&#8217;<br />Capital outflow by Chinese is only a small portion.<br />The money taken away by foreign investments is 60% of China&#8217;s GDP.</p>
<p>Renowned economist He Qinglian discusses<br />“fake foreign investments” in an article.<br />He Qinglian points out that for a long time,<br />a good part of China&#8217;s foreign investments are fake.<br />Money are transferred to overseas by Chinese, who register<br />a company, and the money come back as foreign investments.</p>
<p>According to CCP&#8217;s official information,<br />there are three types of “fake foreign investments.”<br />The first is Chinese funded enterprises with business entities<br />in HK, Macao and foreign countries,<br />who come back to establish “foreign investment”<br />with the need for development.<br />The second is companies originally with domestic funding,<br />which came to invest after registering a company overseas.<br />And the third type is the original domestic company<br />which register as a shell in financial centers overseas.</p>
