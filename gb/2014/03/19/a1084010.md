<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】新型城镇化规划 农民保障如何对接﹖</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2014年03月19日讯】日前，中共中央、国务院印发了有关2014年至2020年的《国家<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/focus/新型城镇化">新型城镇化</a><ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/focus/规划">规划</a>》，并通知各地区部门推进所谓城镇化的要求和目标。而专家表示，中国地方政府不断圈地，把民众的土地给剥夺了，这些失地农民却进不了城，也回不了乡，处境悲惨。另有专家认为，中国的城镇化发展需要转型，农业转移人口落户城镇，不只是一纸户口变更那么简单，保障对接还存在体制机制上的障碍。</p>
<p>中国自1990年代以来，随着工业化、城镇化的加快，大量农村土地被征收、占用、开发，不仅影响农村发展，而且威胁粮食安全，同时，失地农民还面临“种地无田、就业无岗、低保无分”的困境。</p>
<p>中共当局日前提出《<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/focus/新型城镇化">新型城镇化</a><ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/focus/规划">规划</a>》，计划从今年到2020年的6年期间，将“常住人口城镇化率达到60%左右，户籍人口城镇化率达到45%左右，户籍人口城镇化率与常住人口城镇化率差距缩小2百分点左右。”而且还要“努力实现1亿左右农业转移人口和其他常住人口在城镇落户”等。</p>
<p>大陆金融分析师任中道﹕“中共政府的户籍政策，在上世纪50年代，强制的分成农村人口和城市人口。刚开始的时候，农村人只能生活在农村，至于城市的居民的福利待遇，相差很多。日前中共开始新型的城镇化的这样的规划，说是要6年内解决1亿人进城，那么，首先得保证现在民众的相关的福利待遇，医疗、教育、养老等等。”</p>
<p>目前中国农民的失地问题，表现在三个方面，一是﹕土地农转非速度太快，土地征占规模过大，失地农民群体越来越多﹔二是﹕对失地农民补偿太低，不足以解决他们的长远生计﹔三是﹕失地农民的社会保障、及再就业困难。</p>
<p>当前中国的城市退休人员的平均养老金，是新农保的30多倍，显然，较低的新农保养老金，难以满足老年人的基本生活需求。</p>
<p>而中国的城镇化率，近年来每年增长1.3%，每年相应增加的城镇人口约有1800万人，但每年新增就业岗位不到1200万个。</p>
<p>山东临沂农民工卢女士﹕“我觉得大部分老百姓不积极想进城，因为那个进城成本他们都感觉到太高了，当然他们负担不起。你年轻的还可以，年轻人像进工厂、去打工、作工，但是年纪大的人比较悲惨，因为他们的土地也没了，他有土地的话，他最起码能种出粮食来。”</p>
<p>大陆《21世纪网》报导，农业人口转移落户城镇的人均公共成本，以安徽省为例，在10万元左右﹔个人成本不含购房成本，超过1万5000元。而安徽省城、乡居民的“社保”差距，在5:1以上。</p>
<p>任中道﹕“到现在为止，中共完成的只是伪城镇化。即便把土地征用了、开发了、建设了，但是相应的那些人，他处在一个夹心层的状态。什么叫夹心层啊﹖有很多农村的人，4、50岁了，所受的教育、他社会的经验，甚至体力都无法在城市工作，他就是留不在城，回不了乡，留在城里他可能没办法养老，他的医疗、各种保障都没有，也没有自己的住所。”</p>
<p>有专家提出，中国的城镇化发展需要转型。大陆中科院院士陆大道向《21世纪网》表示，城镇就业是决定城镇化速度、城镇规模的主要支撑条件。近年来，中国城镇化速度较快，产业支撑无法跟上，城镇就业岗位的增加赶不上城镇新增人口的增长。 </p>
<p>任中道﹕“现在大家也都看到了，现在中国的经济情况不怎么好，因此，这么多人进城，他的工作经验，包括福利保障等等，它(中共)如果不解决的话，它的城镇化实际上只是纸上谈兵。”</p>
<p>卢女士﹕“它(中共)不怕这1亿多农民变成城市的人的话，其实按照中国的现状，就是耕地被大量的占用，就是因为这个土地越来越少，老百姓没有地可种，没办法，才把这些人变成城市的人。”</p>
<p>当前，中国大陆因征地问题引发的群众抗争越来越烈，而如何让那些处在夹心层的失地农民安度晚年，同样也令人担心。</p>
<p>采访/陈汉 编辑/周平 后制/李勇</p>
<p>The New Urbanization Plan: Where Is Social Security For Farmers?</p>
<p>Recently, the CCP Central Committee and the State Council<br />issued the new national urbanization plan, 2014 through 2020.<br />Local authorities have been notified that they are to promote<br />the so-called urbanization requirements and objectives.<br />Experts say the local governments have deprived people<br />of land.</p>
<p>This movement leads landless farmers into a tragic situation<br />—they can neither enter the city, nor return to the farm.<br />Other experts believe that China&#8217;s urbanization development<br />needs restructuration, as transferring farmers into cities<br />is not as easy as transferring a household registration.<br />There are institutional barriers for protecting<br />the farmers&#8217; social security.</p>
<p>With the acceleration of industrialization and urbanization<br />in China since the 1990s, a large amount of rural land<br />was occupied for economic development,<br />which threatens rural development and food security.<br />At the same time, the landless farmers also face difficulties,<br />such as no land, no employment and no social insurance.</p>
<p>According to the new urbanization plan, the resident<br />population urbanization rate will reach 60 percent,<br />the household population urbanization rate will reach<br />roughly 45 percent, and the gap between the household<br />urbanization rate and the population urbanization rate<br />will reduce by 2 percent.<br />All these targets will be accomplished<br />in the following 6 years.<br />Besides, about 100 million farmers will be settled<br />in the town.</p>
<p>Financial Analyst Ren Zhongdao (China): &#8220;According<br />to the CCP household registration policy in the 1950s,<br />the Chinese people have been forced to divide into<br />two groups—the rural population and urban population.<br />At the beginning, the rural people are required<br />to live in the rural areas.<br />As for the benefits, there are a lot of differences<br />with the residents in the city.<br />The new urbanization plan calls for settling<br />100 million farmers in the town.<br />First of all, the authorities have to ensure welfare for<br />rural dwellers, including medical, education, pension, etc."</p>
<p>At present, the landless issues lead to three problems: first,<br />the land transfer speed is too fast and the land requisition<br />is too big –there are more and more groups of landless farmers;</p>
<p>Second, the compensation for the landless farmers<br />is too low, not enough to solve their long-term livelihood.<br />The third is the social security and re-employment<br />issues for the landless farmers.</p>
<p>According to the new urbanization plan, the current average<br />pension of urban retirees is 30 times more than that of rural retirees.<br />Obviously, it is difficult to meet the basic needs<br />of the elderly living in rural areas.</p>
<p>The urbanization rate in China increased by 1.3 percent<br />per year recently.<br />The corresponding increase in the urban population<br />is about 18 million per year, but the annual increase<br />of jobs is less than 12 million.</p>
<p>Ms. Lu, a migrant worker in Linyi, Shandong: &#8220;I think<br />most people do not want to settle in the city due<br />to the high living cost.<br />Of course they cannot afford it.<br />It could be okay for young people as they could find jobs<br />in the factory.<br />But for the older people, it is miserable –<br />their land is missing.<br />With the land, they could grow food at least."</p>
<p>The 21st Century Network reported that the per capita<br />public costs for transferring the rural population<br />into the town is about 100,000 yuan in Anhui province.<br />The personal cost (excluding housing) is more than<br />15,000 yuan.<br />The social security gap between rural retirees and urban<br />retirees is more than 5:1 in the capital of Anhui rural.</p>
<p>Ren Zhongdao: &#8220;Until now, what the CCP completed<br />is just a pseudo-urbanization.<br />Even the expropriated land was used for economic<br />development, but those related people are moved<br />into a sandwich layer.<br />What does &#8216;sandwich layer&#8217; mean?<br />For many 40 to 50 year-old people living in rural areas,<br />they cannot work in the city according to their<br />educational or social status and even their<br />health condition.<br />They can&#8217;t live in the city, nor return to the rural areas.<br />There is no medical insurance, pension or other<br />social security, including housing."</p>
<p>Some experts suggest that China&#8217;s urbanization<br />development needs restructuration.<br />Lu Dadao, the academician of Chinese Academy<br />of science told the 21st Century Network that<br />urban employment can determine the speed<br />of urbanization.<br />It is the primary condition for sustainment<br />of the urban-scale.<br />In recent years, China&#8217;s urbanization rate is too fast.<br />However, the industry support cannot keep up,<br />so as to the jobs increase rate in urban areas.</p>
<p>Ren Zhongdao: &#8220;Now we find that China&#8217;s<br />economic situation is not very good.<br />Therefore, if the social security for the farmers cannot<br />be solved, then the urbanization will only be talk."</p>
<p>Ms. Lu: &#8220;The CCP is not afraid of transferring these more<br />than 100 million farmers into the city.<br />According to China&#8217;s current situation, a lot of arable lands<br />have been occupied.<br />The land is less and less – there is no land for people<br />to cultivate.<br />Therefore, the CCP has to transfer these people into<br />the cities."</p>
<p>Currently, the struggle caused by land acquisition problems<br />is increasing.<br />Those in the sandwich layer –the landless farmers—deserve<br />to be shown concern. </p>
<p>Interview/Chen Han Edit/Zhou Ping Post-Production/Li Yong</p>
