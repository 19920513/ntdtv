<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】房地产泡沫破裂是中共一劫</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2014年03月24日讯】有诸多迹象显示，中国楼市的繁荣期正在接近尾声。近来到访中国任何一个城市的游客，都会发现，那里有大量建造过多的楼房，这些所谓的“鬼城”，国际媒体认为是中国“楼市繁荣期即将结束的明显证据”。而大陆企业家分析，虽然中国的<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/房地产泡沫.md#1">房地产泡沫</a>越来越大，但是开发商、银行和政府会拚命维系，可是一旦房地产泡沫破裂，中共体制也将随之崩溃。</p>
<p>美国《华尔街日报》3月19号报导说，在中国北部工业城市哈尔滨，从机场进入市区的路上，可以看到郊区有大量“鬼城”，也就是遍布着大量空置高楼的社区。这些空置楼房成为了中国中产阶级不断增长的财富的归宿。在上海和北京这样的大城市，房价在2002至2012年间上涨高达五倍。</p>
<p>大陆企业家王瑛说，中国各个地方的房子供大于求，这是一个很普遍的现象。但是她认为，当局会千方百计的维系目前的状态，因为银行的钱和政府的钱都在这里面。</p>
<p>中国企业家王瑛：“银行把钱贷给了房地产商，房地产商把房子押给了银行。他们总是要千方百计的使这个链条不断，因为一旦断了的话，他们这些人的很多利益都会被砸在里头。因此他们不愿意让它断。实际上地方政府跟这个房子的关系是千丝万缕，所以房地产商、银行，加政府，他们之间会捆绑起来，来维持现有的这个房价。” </p>
<p>王瑛表示，过去三十年，中国的房子，实际上是社会财富重新分配的一个重要工具。</p>
<p>王瑛：“如果当年是打土豪、分田地，来实现了财产重新分配的话，这三十年，实际上最大范围内的财产分配的工具是房子。因为它不是按规则分配的。中国的很多人从无财产到有财产，有一点财产到有不少财产，他们实际上都是透过房子完成的。” </p>
<p>可是现在，中国的很多房地产投资出现了问题。今年1月份，全中国新建住房价格同比涨幅，一年来第一次放缓，成交量也在萎缩。</p>
<p>最近中共当局推出新规，要求处级以上官员登记房产。很多官员纷纷将手中的房产进行处理。</p>
<p>王瑛：“就说这些房子吧，实际上是很多官员拥有的财产，尤其是那些不能够摆到明面上的财产。所以这些人大概对房子的态度，包括他们是不是会继续按照原来的方式拥有更多的房子，其实是影响中国的房地产市场的一个蛮重要的原因。” </p>
<p>而现在人们看到中国的房市泡沫越来越大，当局也很难找到办法来中断这个恶性循环。然而，王瑛指出，他们越是觉得这个危险排除不了，越会把各种各样资源向这里集中。</p>
<p>王瑛：“就特别像这有一个大堤，那边是大江大河，汹涌澎湃，可是堤内有他们的万亩良田。他们的良田不可能搬家，他们只好不断的把那个大堤往高里修。” </p>
<p>这个越来越大的<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/房地产泡沫.md#1">房地产泡沫</a>终究将有破的一天。王瑛表示，她无法预测是哪一天。但是，当那一天来临，那将是中共政权的一大劫。</p>
<p>王瑛：“我是觉得房地产泡沫的崩实际上是这个体制的一个大劫。房地产泡沫一崩，这个体制就再也无法维持下去了。中国的房地产泡沫，已经包含了这个体制几乎全部的内容。因此你让我预期房地产泡沫什么时候崩，几乎就是让我预期中国现在的这个体制什么时候会坍塌？我说我现在做不出判断。” </p>
<p>美国华裔作家章家敦曾说，中共领导人在政治上有不安全感，所以他们痛恨经济下跌。由于他们有资源和权力避免市场修正，所以他们就这么做。但也因为如此，积累的不平衡就越来越大。但是积累的不平衡变大以后，最终无法避免的<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/房市崩盘.md#1">房市崩盘</a>就将变得非常严重。</p>
<p>采访编辑/秦雪 后制/李智远</p>
<p>Doom to the Party: The Collapse of China’s Housing Market</p>
<p>Many indications that the Chinese real estate <br />boom is nearing an end are showing themselves.<br />Tourists who visit any Chinese city will find an excessive <br />amount of newly constructed houses, <br />resulting in ghost towns. </p>
<p>These so-called ghost towns are regarded by foreign <br />media as clear evidence that the housing boom <br />is coming to an end. </p>
<p>A Chinese entrepreneur said despite China’s swelling <br />housing bubble, the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) <br />and its banks, as well as developers are desperately <br />trying to save the market. <br />However, once the bubble finally bursts, the CCP’s <br />regime will subsequently collapse.</p>
<p>The Wall Street Journal reported on March 19 that in Harbin, <br />an industrial city in North China, many ghost towns <br />are visible when heading downtown from the airport. </p>
<p>Ghost towns are mostly unoccupied communities <br />with many high-rise residences. <br />The truth is that much of the savings of China’s middle-class<br />were spent buying those houses. <br />In big cities such as Shanghai and Beijing, housing prices <br />have risen by five times between 2002 and 2012.</p>
<p>Wang Ying, a Chinese entrepreneur, said an excessive <br />housing supply is a phenomena seen all over China. <br />Wang believes that the CCP authorities will still try <br />their best to maintain the current situation <br />because the government and banks have invested <br />so much into the market.</p>
<p>Wang Ying: “Banks make loans to developers and developers <br />mortgage houses to banks. <br />Many local governments are also involved in such business. </p>
<p>Therefore the CCP officials make every effort to keep <br />that connection; otherwise all their interests will be lost. <br />In other words, they don’t want such an interest chain <br />to be cut off. <br />For this reason, developers, banks and governments form <br />an interest group to drive the current housing prices.”</p>
<p>Wang Ying commented that in the past three decades, <br />China’s housing market has been an important tool <br />of the Communist Party in redistribution of social wealth.</p>
<p>Wang Ying, Chinese entrepreneur: “The party used <br />to redistribute social wealth by killing landlords <br />and seizing their land. </p>
<p>In the last three decades, the housing market had become <br />the most important tool to do the same. <br />However, the redistribution did not follow rules <br />commonly understood by the public. <br />Chinese people never owned property for the purpose<br />of acquiring assets, and were unaware assets <br />could be acquired through owning a home.”</p>
<p>Currently, serious problems have developed with many <br />real estate investments. <br />This January, the price of new housing growthshowed <br />a slowdown compared to last January, the first time in a year. <br />Meanwhile, the total trade volume also began to drop.</p>
<p>The CCP authorities recently issued new regulations requiring <br />officials higher than divisional-level to declare all property. <br />Consequently, many officials had to quickly <br />dispose of their houses.</p>
<p>Wang Ying: “For many officials, a large proportion <br />of their assets, especially concealed, are private houses. <br />The future attitude of this group regarding housing <br />purchases, will indeed impact China’s house market.”</p>
<p>Now, as the CCP authority finds it extremely difficult <br />to break the vicious cycle of the swelling housing bubble, <br />many people have taken notice. </p>
<p>Wang Ying believes that the more dangerous the party <br />believes the situation is, the more resources <br />they will pour into the housing market.</p>
<p>Wang Ying:“It (the current bubble) is like a dike holding <br />a flooding river. <br />All their farmland and crops are protected by the dike.</p>
<p> As they cannot move their farmland, the only thing to do <br />is to continuously build the dike up to a higher level.”</p>
<p>Eventually, the swelling housing bubble has to burst. <br />Wang Ying said she does not know when that will happen. <br />However, once that day comes, it will bring a great disaster <br />to the CCP’s regime.</p>
<p>Wang Ying: “I think the collapse of China’s house market <br />is a doom to the party. <br />Once the housing bubble bursts, the party will no longer <br />be able to maintain its regime. <br />All serious problems of the regime have been reflected <br />in China’s housing market. <br />Therefore any prediction of when housing bubble <br />will burst is almost equivalent to talking about <br />when the entire regime will collapse. </p>
<p>Currently I cannot comment further.” </p>
<p>Gordon G. Chang, famous Chinese-American author, <br />once said the CCP leaders hate to see any economic decline, <br />as it makes them feel insecure about their political power. </p>
<p>As they have enough resources and power to affect <br />the market, they use them to avoid a natural recession. <br />For the same reason, the market imbalance<br />keeps heading towards an extreme. <br />After that imbalance exceeds a certain level, <br />the unavoidable collapse of the housing market <br />will finally lead to disastrous consequences <br />to the entire regime.</p>
