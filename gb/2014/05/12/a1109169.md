<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】中国非法外流资金 10年上万亿美元</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2014年05月12日讯】<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/瑞士.md#1">瑞士</a>承诺终结银行保密制之后，受到全球舆论的关注。不过，值得注意的是，在此之前，中共当局从未向瑞士要求提供外逃贪官的银行账户信息，而不是像一般国家那样被瑞士拒绝。实际上，<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/中国.md#1">中国</a>目前已经成为“全球非法资金外流”最多的国家，近10年来已经超过了1万亿美元，而且是以“黑钱洗白”的方式外流。据统计，中国外逃贪官近2万人，携带的资金估算在8千亿到1万5000亿之间。</p>
<p>总部位于巴黎的“经合组织”6号发表公报说，在当天举行的年度部长会议上，包括<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/瑞士.md#1">瑞士</a>在内的34个成员国和巴西、<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/中国.md#1">中国</a>、新加坡等13个国家，共同签署了关于实施“银行间自动交换信息”的标准。这个消息标志着：瑞士不再是中共贪官的“避税天堂”了。</p>
<p>美国华盛顿非营利机构“全球金融诚信”(Global Financial Integrity)组织，去年底发布报告指出，从2002年至2011年，10年之间，中国大陆因逃税、腐败和犯罪等产生的非法资金外流，达1万 800亿美元，是“全球非法资金外流”最多的国家。</p>
<p>而中国外逃的党政官员和国企高管人数，根据中共央行2008年6月撰写的一份有关“贪官向境外转移资产途径”的研究报告说，从上世纪90年代中期以来，中共外逃贪官高达16000人至18000人，携带款项达8000亿元人民币。</p>
<p>陕西国企员工李毓：“一个权力不受控的政府，把国家据为己有的一个权贵集团，举全国之力，被几家或几百家垄断，他们肯定自己也是感觉到，某一天这些财产也不会有保障，所以他们一旦窃取了人民的财富之后，他们就会想办法转移到国外，这是必然的。”</p>
<p>最高法院前院长肖扬在2009年出版的《反贪报告》中，曾引用有关部门的统计指出，从1988年到2002年的15年间，中国的资金外逃额共1913亿5700万美元，年均127亿5700万美元。如果按照当时美元对人民币的汇率，那么外逃资金可能超过了1万5000亿元人民币。</p>
<p>江苏苏州中学教师袁雪成表示，对于资金外逃有三个问题值得关注。</p>
<p>江苏苏州中学教师袁雪成： 我想资金外流首先要有一个规范上的问题，资金怎么被逃出境的？然后如果是实名，比如我们到处都在讲实名，买东西，存钱，买房子，都要实名，那么这些资金外流为什么没有实名？第三个，假如我们某些国家或组织能够查到是谁或者哪些机构的资金外流了，那能不能够追究他们？”</p>
<p>原中共中央体改委干部曹思源对《新唐人》表示，瑞士政府的举措对于贪官的打击具有极大的意义。</p>
<p>原中共中央体改委干部曹思源：“那应该是毁灭性的打击，应该是大快人心的。”</p>
<p>陕西国企员工李毓表示，瑞士银行此举相当于点了贪官的死穴。</p>
<p>李毓：“ 因为大家都是地球村，任何一个地方都希望自己的环境都是谦谦君子，都不可能容忍流氓存在，我认为这是必然的。我只是觉得，瑞士银行的这个举措太晚了。早就应该做。人类是共有的。应该遵守共有的道德观。对于贪官肯定是点了死穴了。”</p>
<p>瑞士政府承诺交出外国人账户的详细资料，对于各国追查贪官或逃税者至关重要，因为瑞士为银行客户保密的传统已有几百年，而且管理着2万2000亿美元的庞大离岸资产。</p>
<p>但是英国《金融时报》的报导说，一些离岸账户户主，已将资金转移到少数几个抵制透明举措的离岸中心。</p>
<p>美国《大西洋》月刊去年11月曾报导说，中国的贪官奸商将大量资金转移到海外，其中大部分被用于购买房产，尤其是位于伦敦、纽约、洛杉矶、悉尼和多伦多的高档住宅。如今高举反腐大旗的中共当局，正在开展全球追讨行动。</p>
<p>去年7月，加拿大和中共当局达成没收、分享和返还赃款、赃物的协议。而去年美国司法部前官员内森尼尔?埃德蒙兹表示，中共官员对在美国的“资产下落很感兴趣”，“美国政府也已表示将与中共官员合作(调查)”。</p>
<p>采访编辑/秦雪 后制/萧宇</p>
<p>More than 1 trillion US dollar funds illegally outflow China in past decade</p>
<p>Switzerland decided to terminate its banking secrecy,<br />and this is highlighted by the global media.<br />But it is notable that till now<br />the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) Authorities<br />have never required the Swiss banks to provide<br />the bank account information of fled corrupt officials.<br />By contrast, many other countries experienced<br />the refusals of Swiss banks.<br />As a matter of fact, China has been ranked<br />as number one country of<br />“funds illegally outflowing in the world”,<br />which is estimated at more than 1 trillion US dollars<br />during the past decade’s money laundering.</p>
<p>According to statistics,<br />China has almost 20000 corrupt officials<br />who successfully fled away overseas<br />and brought away funds<br />between 0.8 and 1.5 trillion US dollars.</p>
<p>The Organization of Economic and Cooperative Development<br />(OECD) headquartered at Paris published<br />an announcement on May 6 and stating that<br />on that day’s Annual Ministerial Conference,<br />34 OECD membership countries<br />and 13 other countries including Brazil, China and Singapore<br />together agreed to sign a treaty “Automatically<br />to exchange information and data among banks”.<br />Such a piece of news signals that Switzerland is no longer<br />the “tax haven” of the CCP’s corrupt officials.</p>
<p>Non-profit organization Global Financial Integrity<br />based in Washington DC,<br />published a report at the end of 2013.</p>
<p>The report shows that from 2002 to 2011,<br />the illicit outflowing of funds as a consequence of tax evasion,<br />corruption and crime reached 1.08 trillion US dollars<br />and China ranked as number one of<br />“funds illegally outflowing in the world”.</p>
<p>Since the mid-1990s, the absconded corrupt officials<br />who took positions in the CCP, Chinese governments<br />and state-owned enterprises had reached<br />between 16,000 to 18,000 and took away 0.8 trillion RMB,<br />according to a study report “The Channels of Transferring<br />Assets to Overseas by Corrupt Officials”<br />published by the CCP’s Central Bank in June of 2008.</p>
<p>A worker of one Shaanxi state-owned enterprise Li Yu:<br />“This is a government with unlimited power.<br />The group of vested interests have monopolised<br />the countries&#8217; industries. They must know<br />that one day such assets will be not secured,<br />so once they have stolen people’s wealth, they had to<br />transfer the funds overseas. This is inevitable.”</p>
<p>Xiao Yang is the former President of the Supreme People’s Court.<br />In the “Anti-Corruption Report” published in 2009,<br />citing the statistics provided<br />by relevant governmental departments,<br />says, during the 15 years from 1988 to 2002,<br />the outflowing of illegal funds reached 191.3 billion US dollars<br />with a yearly average of 12.7 billion US dollars.</p>
<p>In terms of that time’s exchange rate of US dollars to RMB,<br />the outflowing illegal funds<br />could be more than 1.5 trillion RMB.</p>
<p>Yuan Xuecheng, a teacher of Suzhou High School<br />of Jiangsu Province,<br />says there are three issues to be noted.</p>
<p>Yuan Xuecheng：“I think, firstly illegal fund outflows<br />need to be regulated. How did the funds outflow?<br />Secondly, if it is done by real name,<br />for example we all talked about the real-name system<br />for buying something, saving money in banks,<br />buying a property.<br />So why did funds outflow without a real-name system.</p>
<p>Thirdly, if our country or governmental departments<br />can look up whose or which company’s funds outflowed,<br />so can we punish them?”</p>
<p>Mr Cao Siyuan, the ex-official of the previous Commission<br />for Structural Reforms of the CCP Central Committee,<br />told NTD, the new action of Switzerland<br />has an overwhelming significance<br />for attacking the corrupt officials.</p>
<p>Cao Siyuan: “This should be a destructive attack,<br />and should be affording general satisfaction.”</p>
<p>Li Yu says, the action of Swiss banks is equal<br />to dealing a death blow to the corrupt officials.</p>
<p>Li Yu: “because we are all living in a global village.</p>
<p>Every place is hoping its own community are men of honour<br />without the existence of hooligans.<br />I think this is inevitable.<br />I just thought, such an action done by Swiss banks is too late.<br />It should have been applied earlier.<br />We human beings should follow common moral rules.<br />So this is of course to strike a death blow<br />to the corrupt officials.”</p>
<p>The promise of handing over detailed information<br />of all foreigners’ accounts is paramount<br />for chasing corrupt officials or tax evaders.</p>
<p>Swiss banks have maintained banking secrecy<br />for their clients for several hundred years<br />and managed massive offshore assets<br />of around 2.2 trillion US dollars.</p>
<p>But British Financial Times reports,<br />some offshore account owners have transferred funds<br />to limited offshore financial centres<br />which refused to apply transparency policies. </p>
<p>American The Atlantic monthly magazine reported<br />in November of 2013, corrupt Chinese officials<br />and crooked businessmen smuggled billions<br />of dollars overseas.<br />Much of the funds has ended up<br />in overseas real estate—particularly<br />in high-end neighborhoods in London, New York,<br />Los Angeles, Sydney and Toronto.<br />The CCP Authorities are deploying<br />global chasing actions<br />during the current anti-corruption campaign.</p>
<p>In July of 2014, Canada and the CCP Authorities<br />had agreed to seize, share and return the proceeds of crime.<br />Chinese officials “are interested in understanding<br />where the assets are” in the U.S.,<br />and “the U.S. has said it will work with them,”<br />said Nathaniel B. Edmonds ,<br />a former Department of Justice official, last year.</p>
