<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】清点赃款烧坏点钞机 处级官巨贪</h2>
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2014年05月17日讯】由于赃款数量太大，导致点钞机工作时间过长而被烧坏，这样的景象不是戏剧效果，而是出现中国社会的现实画面。最近中国大陆一位主管煤矿项目审批的官员，在被调查中，搜出了家中藏有上亿元人民币，当局调用银行的点钞机来清点时，点钞机真的烧坏了。下面一起来看看。</p>
<p>中共国家能源局煤炭司副司长魏鹏远4月中旬被调查期间，发现他家中藏有上亿元人民币的现金。据报导，当局用16台点钞机来清点，过程中由于点钞机连续工作，使机器过热而当场烧毁了4台。据了解，魏鹏远在能源局煤炭司负责项目改造、煤矿基建的审批和核准。</p>
<p>消息一出，民间一片嘲讽之声。如：“这么多年，货币发行量那么大，却没有出现恶行通货膨胀，原因找到了——大量的现金在贪官的家里，没有流向市场。贪官对控制物价指数做出了重要贡献。”还有人算出，魏鹏远出任国家能源局正处级副司长不到6年，按1亿元来算，平均每天他贪了4万7千多元。 </p>
<p>大陆维权律师隋牧青：“他们认为处级的官员不大，竟然捞这么多钱？在我看来，其实这个数目很小，你想他在那么一个权力部门。他在家里面保存了上亿的现金，这个有一点惊人。因为一般来说，再有钱不太容易家里很多现金，主要是在这里。”</p>
<p>有报导说，如果1亿元都是由100元人民币组成的，那么1亿元人民币就超过1吨重，100米高 ，相当于三十层楼房的高度。</p>
<p>大陆民间反贪人士施釆飞也认为，“魏鹏远式”的中共官员绝不是个别的。</p>
<p>大陆民间反贪人士施釆飞：“这种情况在共产党的领域里很多，这不是偶然，好多好多。不管从高层还是从低层，他们从几百万开始贪污，到上千万，到上亿。层层都在贪污，都在贪。”</p>
<p>施釆飞因举报江苏启东市汇龙镇贪官，侵吞村集体资产，而遭人砍伤，又被关进了精神病院，被施打了毒针。</p>
<p>自2012年以来，有大批中共官员因贪腐罪名落马。而贪官的数量和贪污的赃款只增不减。去年底，国际反<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/focus/腐败">腐败</a>组织“透明国际”公布的年度“全球腐败指数”排行榜，中国名列第80，属于全球腐败比较严重的国家。</p>
<p>隋牧青：“中国这个贪官是抓不完的，有一部分是压力所致，这就相当于土匪交投名状。你这一个部门贪污大家都要贪污，不能说我贪你不贪。有很多人为什么容易被揭发呢？就是因为贪污贪得太少了，你贪的真多的不太容易出事。”</p>
<p>中共反贪，纪委做不到的，靠情妇揭发、和小偷的曝光，反倒做到了。施釆飞证实，中共的反腐无法达到基层。</p>
<p>施釆飞：“（反腐）到了地方上，实际上没有多大的关系。因为地方上根本没有反贪污，他还是跟过去一样。首先它是一个制度，管理也混乱，监督也不够。他既是球员又是裁判员，实际上他不让人家监督。”</p>
<p>中共最高检察机关日前公布，今年1到3月，共立案侦查“贪污贿赂”犯罪案件8222件，涉及10840人。其中贪污贿赂五万元以上、挪用公款十万元以上的大案6759件，占82.2%。</p>
<p>大陆维权律师隋牧青认为，中共这一次的“反腐”是它建政以来最猛烈的一次，但还是解决不了根本问题。 </p>
<p>隋牧青：“这种运动式的反腐，稍微长一点的时间看就不会有成效的。因为中国的<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/focus/腐败">腐败</a>它是个制度性的腐败。他用一种整风式的方式，在这一段时间里让一些人吓得老老实实的，锋头一过一切如旧，只会愈发的厉害。绝对的权力绝对的腐败，一个常态化的反腐只能靠制度。”</p>
<p>隋牧青表示：反贪需要制度的保障，没有宪政和民主的国家里，不可能有一个常态化的、有效的反贪机制。</p>
<p>采访编辑/唐音 后制/孙宁</p>
<p>Money Counting Machines Burn Out <br />During Corruption Investigation of Chinese Official</p>
<p>Recently, a story emerged from China of<br />money bill counters burning out, after<br />overuse in counting embezzled money.</p>
<p>A Deputy Chief of the Chinese Communist<br />Party&#8217;s (CCP) Coal Bureau was found to have<br />hidden over 100 million Yuan in his home.</p>
<p>When the investigators used bill counters to<br />tabulate all the money, some of them burned out.<br />Let&#8217;s see the report.</p>
<p>Wei Pengyuan, a Deputy Chief of the CCP<br />National Energy Administration&#8217;s Coal Bureau,<br />was placed under investigation this April.</p>
<p>Wei was found to have over 100<br />million Yuan in cash at his home.<br />It was reported that the authorities used 16 bill<br />counters to calculate the amount of cash, and<br />four of them burned out due to overheating.</p>
<p>The report said that Wei&#8217;s job is to review and approve<br />renovation and infrastructure projects for the coal bureau.</p>
<p>Many Chinese commented on the<br />humorous nature of the report.<br />Some said &#8220;Inflation has not occurred despite overprinting<br />money, and now we finally know the reason for that.<br />A ton of cash rests at a corrupt officials&#8217;<br />home and never flows into market".<br />&#8220;Our corrupt officials really contribute<br />a lot to controlling the price index".<br />Some calculated that during the less than 6 years when<br />Wei Pengyuan worked as a vice deputy leader of the coal<br />bureau, he embezzled 47,000 Yuan ($7,520) each day.</p>
<p>Sui Muqing, Chinese human rights lawyer:<br />&#8220;Some are astonished that a vice deputy<br />leader was able to steal so much money.<br />However, in my opinion, that is only a relatively small<br />amount, because after all he works in the power sector.<br />The surprising fact is that he had kept<br />over 100 million Yuan in cash at home.<br />Normally very few people keep that large<br />amount of money at home. That&#8217;s my point."</p>
<p>A report said that if this amount was all in<br />100-Yuan bills, it would weigh over a ton.<br />The money pile would reach 100 meters high,<br />about the same as a 30-storey building.</p>
<p>Chinese anti-corruption activist Shi Caifei<br />commented that Wei Pengyuan is not<br />an isolated case among CCP officials.</p>
<p>Shi Caifei: &#8220;There are many CCP officials<br />like that and it did not happen by accident.<br />There are many of them, from<br />high-level to low-level CCP officials.<br />They embezzle amounts from millions to<br />tens of millions, to hundreds of millions.<br />Every level of CCP official is doing the same."</p>
<p>Shi Caifei was previously attacked and injured for<br />reporting on a corrupt official who embezzle collective<br />assets in Huilong town, Qidong city, Jiangsu Province.<br />He was then forcibly abducted to a mental<br />hospital, and was injected with harmful drugs.</p>
<p>Since 2012, a large group of CCP officials<br />have been arrested on corruption charges.<br />Despite this, the number and amount<br />of corruption continues to rise.</p>
<p>By the end of 2013, the anti-corruption<br />organization Transparency International<br />announced its annual ranking corruption index.</p>
<p>China ranks 80th, and belongs to a<br />group of counties with serious corruption.</p>
<p>Sui Fuqing: &#8220;You can never arrest all corrupt<br />officials. Some CCP officials do that under pressure.<br />It is like you have to kill someone as<br />proof of fidelity when you become a bandit.<br />If someone embezzles, others have to follow. You can&#8217;t<br />have corrupt and innocent officials in the same department.<br />Why were some officials exposed?<br />Because they did not embezzle enough, otherwise they<br />won&#8217;t be easily exposed if they have embezzled a lot."</p>
<p>The CCP found a lot of hidden corrupt officials<br />using reports from mistresses and thieves.<br />Shi Caifei confirms though that the CCP&#8217;s anti-corruption<br />campaign never manages to reach the fundamental problem.</p>
<p>Shi Caifei: &#8220;The campaign indeed<br />has little to do with local officials.<br />There are no real anti-corruption actions in<br />local places. Everything is the same as usual.<br />The whole system is chaotic in<br />management, and it lacks supervision.<br />The party is both the player and the referee, and it indeed<br />never allows independent observers to monitor its actions."</p>
<p>The CCP&#8217;s supreme procuratorate<br />recently made an announcement.<br />They had investigated 8,222 corruption and<br />bribery cases between January and March.<br />10,840 individuals were found to have been involved.</p>
<p>Among those cases, 82.2 percent, or 6,759 cases involve<br />an amount of embezzlement over 50,000 Yuan ($8,000).</p>
<p>Chinese human rights lawyers Sui Muqing comments<br />that the CCP is currently running it&#8217;s most serious<br />anti-corruption campaign since it took power.<br />However, it still cannot solve the problem.</p>
<p>Sui Muqing: &#8220;In the long run, any<br />&#8216;campaign&#8217; or &#8216;movement&#8217; will not work.<br />The corruption inside the CCP is a result of the whole regime.<br />Party leaders use rectification campaign to frighten some<br />corrupt officials, but they will return to their old ways.<br />They may even become worse after the campaign<br />ends. Absolute power leads to absolute corruption.<br />Consistent anti-corruption can only<br />be supported by a good system."</p>
<p>Sui Muqing said anti-corruption can only be<br />effective under protection of a good authority.<br />In a state without constitutionalism and democracy, there can<br />never be an effective and consistent anti-corruption system.</p>
