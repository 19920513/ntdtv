<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】在华外企联手 质疑“反垄断调查”</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2014年09月08日讯】最近，中共频频使用《反垄断法》，对众多知名外资企业进行调查。在只能默默承受中共当局的突袭办公室、漫长的调查，以及高额罚款之后，有迹象显示<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/在华外企.md#1">在华外企</a>打算联手反抗。继上个月“中国欧盟商会”发表声明，日前，“美中贸易全国委员会”也发布报告，质疑中共反垄断程序的正当性。</p>
<p>9月3号，代表200多家在华美商的“美中贸易全国委员会”(US-China Business Council) 发表报告指出，中共当局最近频繁以“反垄断”为由，不按正当程序，对<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/在华外企.md#1">在华外企</a>施加压力。</p>
<p>报告说，中共反垄断执法机构逼迫被调查企业承认违法行为，但不出示相关证据，也不给这些企业就相关指控作出解释的机会，甚至不说明调查理由。</p>
<p>报告还说，一些企业透露，在执法人员对其办公室进行突访前，它们没有得到联系法律顾问的机会，而在后续可能开展的冗长谈判中，它们的律师也被排除在外。</p>
<p>原北京大学法律系讲师王天成：“这个委员会提出的这些要求，从法律的角度上来说，当然是正当的，中国政府要反垄断，它应该遵循正当的法律的程序，应该要有必要的证据，应该要有善意的程序听取解释，这是法治所要求的。”</p>
<p>据了解，到目前为止，至少30家外资企业受到中共的所谓“<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/反垄断调查.md#1">反垄断调查</a>”。7月28号，美国电脑软件巨头“微软”在北京、上海等地的4个办事处，遭到中共国家工商总局突击调查。在此之前，中共发改委曾对世上最大手机芯片制造商“高通”发起反垄断调查，“高通”被指控滥用市场支配地位非法牟利。</p>
<p>8月4号，跨国汽车公司“奔驰”(Mercedes-Benz)位于上海的办事处，也遭到<ahref="https://github.com/19920513/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/反垄断调查.md#1">反垄断调查</a>。而“克莱斯勒”(Chrysler)和“奥迪”(Audi)两家企业，已被查明存在垄断行为，其中一汽-大众奥迪的罚金初步定为18亿元人民币，如果18亿罚单成真，将创下中共反垄断罚金最高记录。</p>
<p>8月20号，中共发改委宣布，对12家日本汽车配件生产商，罚款12.4亿元人民币。</p>
<p>美中贸易全国委员会主席傅强恩(John Frisbie)指出，中共执法机构的正当程序、透明度，以及赔偿和罚款的裁定方法，都需要改善。</p>
<p>上个月，代表1800家欧洲在华企业的“中国欧盟商会”(European Union Chamber of Commerce in China)针对中共当局的反垄断调查发表声明说：“在未进行充分听证的前提下，有关部门通过带有恐吓性的行政手段迫使企业接受惩罚和治理，这类现象值得高度警惕。”</p>
<p>声明指出：“诸如告知企业不要对调查者提出质疑和挑战、不要在审讯环节雇佣律师或求助其政府、所属商会等行为，都与最佳讼程程序背道而驰。”</p>
<p>旅美中国社会问题研究人士张健：“外资企业在华受到一些优待之后，中共各地方政府也露出了狰狞的面孔，‘养肥了猪，就要杀了过年’用这种方式来对所有的外资企业进行整肃，而且以黑道的方式，不讲理的方式，公然践踏了法律，也践踏了反垄断法的公平、公开、公正的三公原则。”</p>
<p>据《路透社》8月21号报导，中共发改委官员徐新宇在近期一次会议上，向“通用电气”和“西门子”等约30家外企施压，要求他们坦白垄断行为，并警告他们不要聘请外部律师来对抗监管机构的指控。一名与会消息人士透露，徐新宇对在场企业说：“如果你想对抗，我可以将罚金翻一倍或两倍。”</p>
<p>《纽约时报》指出，外资企业通常缺少中国竞争对手所拥有的政治背景，一旦成为打击目标，往往会保持沉默。</p>
<p>王天成：“如果你去抗议，你可能会受到惩罚，会受到报复，所以他们就会忍气吞声。不过还好，毕竟是外企，有自己的国家，在自己的国家还有一些组织，就像刚才说的委员会，给它们发出声音。”</p>
<p>旅美中国社会问题研究人士张健指出，最应该被反垄断调查的，是中国所有的国有企业，那些遭到中共调查的外企，很有可能会联手，把中共这种不公平对待，以及违反法律程序的种种行径，告到国际法庭。</p>
<p>采访/易如 编辑/陈洁 后制/李勇</p>
<p>Foreign Companies Resist the CCP&#8217;s &#8220;Anti-Monopoly" Raids</p>
<p>Recently, the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) has used <br />Anti-Monopoly law to investigate a number of renowned <br />foreign companies. </p>
<p>After silent tolerance of the CCP&#8217;s surprise raids, lengthy <br />investigation and heavy fines, those foreign companies <br />finally made some resistance. </p>
<p>Following European Union Chamber of Commerce in China, <br />US-China Business Council also made a statement that <br />questions legitimacy of the CCP&#8217;s “Anti-Monopoly” moves.</p>
<p>On Sep. 3, US-China Business Council (USCBC), a group <br />on behalf of over 200 US companies in China, issued a report. </p>
<p>The report said the CCP had been pressing foreign <br />companies in the name of &#8220;Anti-Monopoly" without <br />due procedure.</p>
<p>According to the report, the CCP&#8217;s anti-monopoly organ <br />had forced companies to admit their illegal acts. </p>
<p>However, the officers did not show evidence or give <br />companies any chance to defend themselves. </p>
<p>The companies were not even told why they <br />were investigated.</p>
<p>The report also quoted sources that the companies were <br />not given any chance to contact legal counselors <br />before surprise raids. </p>
<p>In the following negotiation that could possibly be very <br />lengthy, their lawyers were also left out of the discussion.</p>
<p>Wang Tiancheng, former lecturer in law at Peking </p>
<p>University:"The Council&#8217;s demands are definitely legitimate. <br />The CCP should follow the right procedures to make <br />anti-monopoly investigations. <br />They should provide necessary evidence as well as <br />considerate hearing programs. <br />These things are demanded by the rule of law."</p>
<p>At least 30 foreign companies were reportedly subjected to <br />the CCP&#8217;s so-called &#8220;Anti-Monopoly" investigation. </p>
<p>On July 28, four Microsoft offices in Beijing or Shanghai <br />were abruptly raided by the State Administration <br />for Industry and Commerce. </p>
<p>Shortly before that, the CCP also investigated Qualcomm, <br />the biggest maker of smart phone chips, <br />on anti-monopoly charges. </p>
<p>The CCP claimed Qualcomm had made unlawful profits <br />by abusing its leading role in the market.</p>
<p>On Aug. 4, Mercedes-Benz&#8217;s agency in Shanghai was also <br />raided by anti-monopoly officers. </p>
<p>Chrysler and Audi were alleged by the CCP to violate <br />anti-monopoly law. </p>
<p>FAW&#8217;s Audi was preliminarily demanded a fine of <br />1.8 billion Yuan ($293M). </p>
<p>This would be the largest ever anti-monopoly fine <br />in China if it becomes true.</p>
<p>On Aug. 20, the CCP&#8217;s National Development and Reform <br />Commission (NDRC) also announced that 12 Japanese <br />makers of auto parts would be fined 1.24 billion Yuan ($200M).</p>
<p>John Frisbie, USCBC president, said the CCP needs to <br />improve its &#8220;due process, transparency and the <br />methodology for determining remedies and fines."</p>
<p>Last month, European Union Chamber of Commerce in China <br />also made a statement on the CCP&#8217;s Anti-Monopoly raids, <br />on behalf of 1800 European companies in China. </p>
<p>The statement said &#8220;numerous alarming anecdotal accounts <br />from a number of sectors that administrative intimidation <br />tactics are being used to impel companies to accept <br />punishments and remedies without full hearings."</p>
<p>The statement said &#8220;practices such as informing companies <br />not to challenge the investigations, bring lawyers to <br />hearings or involve their respective governments or <br />chambers of commerce are contrary to best practices."</p>
<p>Zhang Jian, scholar of China social affairs:"After giving <br />preferential treatment, the CCP&#8217;s local governments finally <br />revealed their ferocious faces. <br />They are crushing foreign companies like pigs that are well <br />fed for New Year&#8217;s feast. <br />When doing that, they acted like mafias, ignoring law <br />and the principles of anti-monopoly law which are fairness, <br />openness and justice."</p>
<p>Reuters reported on Aug. 21 that Xu Xinyu, an NDRC officer, <br />intimidated about 30 foreign companies like General <br />Electric and Siemens to admit their monopoly acts <br />in a recent meeting. </p>
<p>He also warned them not to resist by hiring lawyers. </p>
<p>A source at the meeting said, Xu told those companies <br />&#8220;I will double fine if anyone attempts to resist."</p>
<p>New York Times commented that, unlike their Chinese <br />competitors, most foreign companies do not have any <br />political background in China. </p>
<p>Therefore they usually stay silent even under <br />harsh crackdown.</p>
<p>Wang Tiancheng:"You may be punished or even retaliated <br />against if making any protests. <br />That&#8217;s why those companies submit to humiliation. <br />But after all foreign companies have native countries. <br />Organizations from their own countries can speak publicly <br />for them."</p>
<p>Zhang Jian said it is all the CCP&#8217;s state-owned companies <br />that should be subject to anti-monopoly investigation. </p>
<p>After being raided by the CCP, those foreign companies <br />may unite to appeal to international courts against <br />the CCP&#8217;s unfair treatment and violation of due procedure.</p>
